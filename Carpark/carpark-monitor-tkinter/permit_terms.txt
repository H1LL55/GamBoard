CCCU STAFF PARKING PERMIT TERMS AND CONDITIONS

Please read these terms before submitting your application.

1. General permit information
- A parking permit is required for staff who wish to park on University premises and who meet the permit criteria.
- Parking remains subject to limited availability. A permit does not guarantee that a space will be available.
- Permit-holder parking is first come, first served, so on busy days you may need to use other local parking options.
- The University prioritises parking for colleagues most in need and supports sustainable travel wherever possible.

2. Eligibility and evidence
- Staff applications should normally fall within an agreed eligibility category, such as:
  * Blue Badge holder
  * mobility or significant health condition
  * registered carer or qualifying caring responsibility
  * responsibility for a child aged 11 or under
  * essential business use requiring travel at least 3 times per week
  * Friday-only permit where applicable
- Supporting evidence may be required depending on the category selected.
- Essential business users must have business-use insurance for their vehicle.
- Some employment categories may not be eligible for a standard permit.

3. Canterbury distance rule
- Staff living within 3 miles of the Canterbury campus are normally not eligible for a permit unless an exemption category applies, such as Blue Badge, mobility / health grounds, or essential business use.

4. Application and approval process
- Submitting this form does not mean your permit has been approved.
- New staff applications are reviewed by the relevant Head of School / Department and then the relevant SMT member before being sent for processing.
- Relevant supporting evidence should be supplied as part of the approval process where required.
- If approved, the permit is usually available for collection from the relevant campus reception within around 5 working days of receipt by the Estates and Facilities team.

5. Using the permit
- The permit must be displayed clearly when parking on campus.
- If you are parked in an accessible bay, a valid Blue Badge must also be displayed.
- You must only park in marked parking spaces.
- Disabled spaces are only for valid Blue Badge holders.
- Visitor spaces are only for visitors.
- The permit should not be used on days you are not working on campus.

6. Parking charges and enforcement
- Parking without the correct permit, parking in the wrong bay, or failing to follow the parking rules may result in a Parking Charge Notice.
- The parking charge may be £70, reduced to £35 if paid within 14 days.
- Persistent misuse of accessible bays or serious breaches of the parking arrangements may lead to further action, including removal of the permit.
- Rude, abusive, or aggressive behaviour towards staff dealing with parking may be referred to managers as a possible disciplinary matter.

7. Charges, replacement, and cancellation
- Current permit charges may be suspended for the relevant permit year, but charging may be reintroduced in a future period in line with University policy.
- If charges are reintroduced, staff may be contacted by University email and given notice.
- A lost or defaced permit may be replaced, but a replacement charge may apply.
- If you wish to cancel a permit, you must return the permit and complete the relevant cancellation process.

8. Vehicle and permit changes
- If your vehicle registration changes, you should contact the Facilities Helpdesk so the permit details can be updated.
- If your circumstances change and you are no longer eligible, you should notify the Facilities Helpdesk.

9. Electric vehicle charging
- Electric vehicle charging bays are for charging only, not general parking.
- Separate charging rules may apply, including time limits and charging requirements.
- Users of EV charging facilities must follow the University's EV charging arrangements.

10. Final acknowledgement
By ticking the acceptance box and submitting this application, you confirm that:
- the information you have entered is true to the best of your knowledge;
- you have read and understood these parking permit terms;
- you understand that submitting the form does not guarantee approval or a parking space; and
- you agree to follow the University parking rules and permit conditions.
